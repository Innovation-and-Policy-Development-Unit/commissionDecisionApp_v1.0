\subsection{1. Purpose}\label{purpose}

This document provides a cost quotation and justification for the
procurement of Artificial Intelligence (AI) API services to power three
planned AI features within the Public Service Commission's Submission
and Commission Decision Management System (SCDMS).

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{2. Background}\label{background}

The SCDMS is a full-stack web application developed for the Public
Service Commission (PSC) of Vanuatu that manages the complete lifecycle
of personnel decisions --- from ministry submission through PSC
assessment, commission sittings, formal minutes, and post-decision
implementation tracking.

As part of Phase 2 development, three AI-powered features have been
designed and are ready for implementation. These features require access
to an AI language model API provided by \textbf{Anthropic PBC}, a
leading AI safety company headquartered in San Francisco, USA.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{3. Proposed AI Features}\label{proposed-ai-features}

\subsubsection{Feature 1 --- Automated Feedback
Triage}\label{feature-1-automated-feedback-triage}

\textbf{What it does:} When system users submit feedback reports (bugs,
workflow issues, suggestions), the AI automatically: - Generates a
one-sentence summary - Assigns a severity level (Low / Medium / High /
Critical) - Categorises the report (Bug / Feature Request /
Legal-Compliance / General Inquiry) - Translates non-English text to
English for the development team

\textbf{Benefit:} Reduces manual effort in reviewing and prioritising
feedback. Ensures no critical issues are missed.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsubsection{Feature 2 --- Meeting Minutes AI
Drafting}\label{feature-2-meeting-minutes-ai-drafting}

\textbf{What it does:} After a Commission sitting, the secretary uploads
the audio recording. The system: 1. Transcribes the audio to text (via a
speech-to-text service) 2. Sends the transcript to the AI, which
extracts speakers, agenda items discussed, decisions made, and action
items 3. Auto-populates a structured draft of the official Minutes
document

\textbf{Benefit:} Significantly reduces the time the PSC Secretary
spends drafting minutes from recordings. The secretary reviews, edits,
and signs off on the AI-generated draft --- full human oversight is
maintained.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsubsection{Feature 3 --- Natural Language Chatbot
Assistant}\label{feature-3-natural-language-chatbot-assistant}

\textbf{What it does:} A conversational assistant embedded in the SCDMS
portal that allows authorised staff to ask plain-English questions about
the system's data. Examples: - \emph{``Which submissions from the
Ministry of Finance are still under assessment?''} - \emph{``What
decisions were made in the last commission sitting?''} - \emph{``Which
implementation tasks are overdue this month?''} - \emph{``Show me all
compliance submissions received this year.''}

\textbf{Benefit:} Eliminates the need for staff to navigate multiple
screens to find information. Improves efficiency and reduces training
burden for new users.

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{7. Data \& Privacy
Considerations}\label{data-privacy-considerations}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  All data sent to the Anthropic API is processed in-transit (HTTPS
  encrypted)
\item
  Anthropic does not use API inputs to train their models (per their API
  usage policy)
\item
  No personally identifiable information (PII) beyond what is necessary
  will be sent --- prompts will be structured to pass anonymised or
  role-level context only
\item
  Data residency: Anthropic processes data in the United States
\end{itemize}

\begin{center}\rule{0.5\linewidth}{0.5pt}\end{center}

\subsection{8. Summary \& Recommendation}\label{summary-recommendation}

The three AI features proposed for the SCDMS will materially improve the
efficiency of PSC secretariat operations, reduce administrative burden
on staff, and enhance the usability of the system for all ministry
users.

The cost is minimal --- estimated at \textbf{USD \$10--20 per month}
(approximately \textbf{VT 1,200--2,400 per month}) --- and the service
carries no contract, no lock-in, and is capped at an approved spending
limit to eliminate financial risk.

\textbf{It is recommended that the Director, IPDU, approve a monthly AI
API budget of USD \$25.00 (approximately VT 3,000) to be reviewed at the
end of the 2026 financial year.}
